Frequently asked questions

Quick hearing aid answers

Why is my hearing aid not producing sound?

Check the battery or charger, clean the dome or earmold, replace the wax guard, and inspect the microphone openings. If it still does not work, the device may need professional service.

Why does my hearing aid whistle?

Whistling is often caused by a loose fit, earwax, a damaged dome or earmold, excessive volume, or an object covering the ear. A professional fit check may be needed.

Why can I hear people but not understand them?

Hearing loss can affect clarity as well as volume. Background noise, hearing aid programming, speech discrimination, and listening environment can all play a role.

How often should hearing aids be replaced?

Many people use hearing aids for about five to seven years, though care, technology needs, hearing changes, and repair history matter more than a fixed number.

Does Medicare cover hearing aids?

Coverage depends on the specific Medicare plan. Original Medicare generally does not cover routine hearing aids, while some Medicare Advantage plans may offer a hearing benefit.
